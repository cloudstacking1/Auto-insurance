FAQ's

Commercial auto insurance is a policy designed to cover vehicles that are used for business purposes. This type of insurance protects businesses from financial losses due to accidents, vehicle damage, theft, or liability claims. It can cover company cars, trucks, and fleets, as well as employees using their personal vehicles for work-related tasks.

Commercial auto insurance is essential for any business that uses vehicles for operations, whether you own one car or a fleet of trucks. Without proper coverage, your business could be exposed to significant financial risk in case of accidents, property damage, or injuries involving your vehicles. It ensures that your assets, employees, and business reputation are protected.

The main difference is that personal auto insurance covers vehicles used for personal reasons, while commercial auto insurance is designed for vehicles used for business purposes. Commercial policies offer more extensive coverage, including liability and property damage protection tailored to business needs. Additionally, personal auto insurance may not cover accidents or damages incurred while using your vehicle for work-related tasks, making commercial auto insurance essential for business owners.
